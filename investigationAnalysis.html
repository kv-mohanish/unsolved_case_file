Primary Questions for Investigation
Who committed the crime? — Identify the perpetrator and establish the convergence of motive, means, and opportunity that places them above all other suspects. A partial case is not a conviction.
How was it executed? — Reconstruct the precise sequence: acquisition of compound, preparation, the specific moment of administration, and the method used to avoid detection in a room full of people.
Why that night? — The timing is not incidental. The act was triggered by a specific, fixed deadline. Identify what that deadline was and which suspect faced it most acutely.
What are the others hiding? — Not every lie is a murder. Determine which suspects are concealing guilt and which are concealing something else — embarrassment, complicity, or something they witnessed but have not disclosed.
