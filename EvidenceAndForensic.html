Classification
Based on the totality of forensic findings, this case is formally classified as Confirmed Homicide — Deliberate Toxic Ingestion. The mechanism of administration is consistent with premeditated action by an individual with direct access to the victim's drink, knowledge of the compound's effects, and sufficient composure to administer it without detection in a social setting.
Forensic Implication for Suspect Assessment
The compound's restricted nature narrows the field. The act required: (1) advance acquisition of a non-trivial substance, (2) preparation in a concentrated form, (3) access to the victim's specific glass during the administration window, and (4) the ability to act without drawing attention. Investigators should weigh which suspect satisfies all four criteria — not merely one or two of them.
The absence of struggle or visible distress indicates the victim trusted the individual who administered the substance — or simply did not see them do it. Either scenario is consistent with someone whose proximity to him was expected and unremarkable.
