Case Summary
Overview
On the night of 14th March, Rohan Verma hosted a private end-of-semester gathering at Wisteria Villa — a rented off-campus property chosen for its isolation from the main college campus. The invitation list was small and deliberate: five people who shared varying degrees of proximity to Rohan — some by friendship, some by professional dependency, and at least one, it is now suspected, by fear.

What was presented as a celebration concealed an environment of escalating pressure. In the 72 hours prior to the event, Rohan had sent a series of threatening communications to multiple individuals within the group. The content of these messages, now partially recovered, makes clear that he intended to use private information — gathered over months — as leverage. For at least one recipient, the threat was existential. The party was not merely a gathering. For someone in that room, it was a deadline.
The Atmosphere That Night
Attendees who arrived early describe the first hour as deceptively ordinary. Music was playing. Drinks were being served. Conversations were surface-level. But those present note that Rohan was agitated in a way they hadn't seen before — distracted, checking his phone repeatedly, and at one point stepping outside alone for nearly fifteen minutes.

At 11:45 PM, that veneer collapsed. A confrontation between Rohan and Megha — his former girlfriend — escalated into a full public argument. Multiple attendees describe Rohan as "cruel" and "calculated" in his delivery, as though the argument had been prepared in advance. Megha's response was audibly threatening. The group fractured. Some moved outside. Some retreated to corners. The atmosphere, according to one account, "felt like a fuse had been lit."

It was in this charged context — fractured attention, scattered positions, rising hostility — that the killing took place.
Incident Reconstruction
10:30 PM — Gathering begins. All five suspects arrive within a 40-minute window. Initial atmosphere described as relaxed. Rohan reportedly greets each guest privately before the group assembles.
11:00 PM — Kabir and Rohan are observed in a private conversation near the rear terrace. Witnesses describe the exchange as "tense." Both deny a confrontation occurred at this time.
11:45 PM — Public argument erupts between Rohan and Megha. Overheard by all attendees. Rohan references specific private information — the nature of which witnesses decline to name. Megha makes an audible threat: "You'll regret this." Multiple guests deliberately withdraw.
12:00 AM — Group reassembles for cake cutting. All five suspects confirmed present by photographic evidence. Rohan is described as visibly relaxed at this moment — "as if the argument hadn't happened."
12:05 AM — A threatening message is sent from Rohan's device to a suspect. The message references exposing sensitive personal information "first thing tomorrow morning." A reply is received from another device at 12:06 AM. Both messages were subsequently deleted and later recovered via forensic extraction.
12:20 AM — Power outage. Duration: approximately 6–7 minutes. All indoor surveillance, ambient lighting, and the villa's CCTV system are taken offline. Individual positions are entirely unverified during this window. One witness reports hearing movement near the service corridor. Another reports seeing a figure re-enter from the parking area as lights returned.
12:30 AM — Drinks distributed. A photograph timestamped 12:32 AM documents an individual near the drinks tray in the main hall. The identity of that individual is contested. Forensic analysis later confirms a toxic substance was introduced into the victim's glass at or around this time.
1:00 AM — Several guests begin to depart. Rohan is seen by two individuals — accounts of his condition at this point differ significantly. One describes him as "quieter than usual." The other describes him as "totally fine."
1:15 AM — Last confirmed sighting of the victim — observed alone in the main hall by a single witness. No further verified interactions established.
2:10 AM — Rohan Verma discovered unconscious in his private bedroom. No external injuries. No signs of forced entry. No evidence of a struggle. Emergency services contacted. Cause of death reclassified to homicide following toxicology.
The 12:20–12:30 AM window is the primary interval of interest. No suspect has provided an account that is both internally consistent and independently corroborated during this period.

Personal Background
Rohan Verma occupied a position of disproportionate informal authority within his immediate social and academic network. He was widely regarded as charismatic and perceptive — a person who understood what others wanted and was willing to exploit that knowledge. Peers describe him as "strategically charming": warm when he needed something, cold when he didn't. In the months before his death, that balance had shifted decisively toward the latter.

Rohan had, over the course of his final year, assembled what can only be described as a private ledger of personal information on the people closest to him. The method of collection is unclear — but the pattern is documented. He gathered private disclosures shared in confidence, financial irregularities, romantic indiscretions, and professional failures — each held as potential leverage. His behaviour in the weeks before the gathering had escalated from subtle pressure to explicit threat.
Documented Behavioural Patterns
Ongoing communication pattern consistent with blackmail — threatening to expose sensitive personal material unless specific demands were met; documented across multiple recipients within the same social group
At least three separate threatening communications issued in the 72 hours before the gathering — each to a different individual; each referencing a different piece of private information
Used social gatherings as environments for control — settings where witnesses were present prevented targets from reacting visibly, giving him a tactical advantage
Professional interference documented in at least two cases — used back-channel influence to redirect academic and career opportunities away from associates he wanted to keep dependent on him
In at least one case, had knowledge of a matter that, if disclosed, would have had legal — not merely reputational — consequences for the recipient
The Information He Held
The precise nature of the information Rohan possessed about each individual has not been fully established. What is known — from partially recovered digital records and corroborating accounts — is that each of the five individuals present that night had a documented or strongly implied reason to fear disclosure. The motive pool is wide. The killer's identity is not determined by who had a reason to act — almost everyone did. It is determined by who had the means, the access, and the specific pressure applied that night.
Last Confirmed Status
Last confirmed interaction: approximately 12:30 AM, during the drink distribution phase. No verified contact with any individual established after this point. Discovered unconscious at 2:10 AM in his private bedroom. Toxicology confirms ingestion of a controlled toxic compound — concentration consistent with a lethal deliberate dose administered via his drink.
